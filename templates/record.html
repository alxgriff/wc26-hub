How to read this page

Two independent scorecards. They measure different things and can disagree on the same match — that is the method working, not a contradiction.

The Calls · Brier

How good our forecast was — the probability we put on each result before kickoff, graded by Brier score once the match is settled. Lower is better: 0 is perfect, 0.667 a coin flip, 2 as wrong as possible. This measures being right, not betting.

The Bets · Units & CLV

Paper picks recorded only when our probability beats the market price — scored in units (flat 1u) and closing-line value. A pick can lose while the call was right: we may rate the favorite to win yet bet the other side because the book underpriced it. D1 is the case in point — we called the USA, who won, but Paraguay was the value bet.

The Shadow Book · risky calls

The calls where our model and the market severely disagree — the edge is so large it's usually our error (a ratings quirk), occasionally a real overlay. Too risky to stake, so we track them on paper to learn who's right. Hypothetical units only, kept out of the units record above.
