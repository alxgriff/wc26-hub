The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played, the standings are settled, and the bracket for the knockout rounds is now fixed. After nearly six weeks of football spread across three countries, the tournament pauses for a day before the round of 32 begins in earnest — a moment to take stock rather than preview the next result.

What the group stage produced was a competition with fewer certainties than the seedings implied. Some fancied sides stumbled through, others were eliminated earlier than expected, and the margins that separated teams in several groups were fine enough to make the final day fixtures genuinely anxious occasions. The knockout rounds offer no such complexity: one match, one result, no second chances. The schedule resumes tomorrow, and the arithmetic gets considerably simpler from here.
