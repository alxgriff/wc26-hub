The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played, the standings are settled, and the bracket for the knockout rounds is now fixed. After 24 days of football spread across three countries, the tournament pauses on this Sunday before the round of 32 begins in earnest — a brief stillness that the competition has not really allowed itself until now.

It is a reasonable moment to take stock. Some nations that arrived with considerable expectations will not be travelling to the next venue; others qualified quietly and may prove more difficult to remove than their group-stage records suggest. The knockout format is unforgiving in ways the group stage is not, and a single bad hour can end a campaign that survived three matches of attrition. What the next phase will not offer, in any case, is the comfort of a second chance.
