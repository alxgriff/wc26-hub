The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played across 35 days, the standings are settled, and the bracket for the knockout rounds is now fixed. Thursday offers no football — a deliberate pause before the tournament shifts register entirely, trading the cumulative arithmetic of points and goal difference for the blunter logic of a single game. It is, in its quiet way, a useful moment: time for the dust to settle on some genuinely surprising group outcomes, for travel arrangements to be made, and for the kind of collective breath-holding that precedes elimination football. The round of 32 begins Friday, and from here every team that loses goes home. There will be no second chances, no safety nets, no comfort in a draw. Thirty-two teams remain. Thirty-one of them will eventually lose.
