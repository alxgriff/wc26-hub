The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played, the standings are final, and 32 teams have been reduced to 16 — though for roughly half of those 16, the margin between progress and elimination was uncomfortable enough to suggest the knockout rounds will not be kind to anyone who plays cautiously. Monday is a rest day in the strictest sense: no fixtures, no whistles, nothing to parse in real time. What it offers instead is the chance to look squarely at what the group stage produced before the round of 16 begins and the tournament's whole character changes. Single-elimination football has a way of making everything that came before it feel like prologue. Starting Tuesday, there are no second chances, no goal-difference arithmetic to hide behind, and no draws that feel like acceptable outcomes. The bracket is set.
