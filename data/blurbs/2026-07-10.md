The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played across 30 days, the standings are settled, and the tournament now pauses before the knockout rounds begin. It is the kind of day that exists between things — too late for recriminations about what went wrong in the groups, too early for the focused dread that accompanies a single-elimination bracket. Fans of the 32 teams that arrived with ambitions have had time enough to either book onward travel or cancel it. What comes next will be played on different terms entirely, where a bad hour cannot be smoothed out by a third game. The round of 32 begins soon, and the margin for the sort of result that can be explained away shrinks to nothing.
