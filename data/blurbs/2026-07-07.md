Seventy-two matches across the group stage, and the last of them is done. Every nation now knows its fate — which sixteen advance, which thirty-two go home, and in what order the survivors will meet in the round of sixteen beginning later this week. The numbers have been crunched, the goal differences tallied, the tiebreakers applied with varying degrees of controversy. What the group stage rarely provides is tidy conclusions, and this edition has been no different: there were results that surprised, margins that flattered, and a handful of sides that looked rather better on paper than they did on grass. Today is a pause between the noise of the groups and the sharper, more final arithmetic of the knockouts — a day for reading the bracket carefully, because from here a single loss ends everything.
