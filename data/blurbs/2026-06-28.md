The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played across 18 days, the standings are settled, and the bracket for the knockout rounds is now fixed. What began with 48 teams in a dozen cities has been whittled down to the 32 who advance, and the rest have already begun the long journey home. There is a certain stillness to a day like this — no kick-offs, no late equalisers to scramble the tables — just the arithmetic of what happened sitting undisturbed on the page. The round of 32 opens tomorrow, and with it the tournament becomes a different competition entirely: single elimination, no second chances, no comfort in a draw. For now, though, the day belongs to transit lounges and quiet reflection on what might have been.
