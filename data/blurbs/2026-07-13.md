The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played, the tables are settled, and the bracket for the knockout rounds is set — which means Monday offers something the previous four weeks have not: a day with nothing on the schedule and no result to chase. That stillness is its own kind of news. Thirty-two teams arrived for the tournament; the ones still standing now know exactly who they face and, more pressingly, who among their rivals survived to join them in the draw. The work of the group stage — the points traded, the goal differences nursed, the late equalizers that reshuffled entire sections of the bracket — is finished. What follows is single-elimination football, where the margin for error disappears entirely. Tuesday will bring the first matches of the round of 32. For now, a pause.
