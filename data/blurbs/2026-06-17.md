Day 7 brings four group-stage openers across Groups K and L, and the range of competitive tension on offer is considerable. Portugal carry an 80 percent consensus probability into their midday kickoff against DR Congo, making it the day's clearest mismatch on paper, though a recorded spread pick at minus-1.5 reflects the difference between backing a winner and backing a margin. England and Croatia, who shared one of the tournament's more celebrated group-stage meetings eight years ago, sit almost evenly split by comparison — England favored at 58 percent, Croatia at 18, with a quarter of the probability mass still sitting on a draw, which is as honest an expression of uncertainty as the markets offer. Ghana and Panama close the afternoon slate with the consensus flipped toward Panama at 53 percent, an unusual position for a side from the African confederation. Uzbekistan and Colombia round out the evening, Colombia the heavy 76 percent favorites. In the third-place standings, Czechia hold eighth on goal difference over Ecuador, a margin thin enough that today's results in both groups feed directly into that arithmetic.
