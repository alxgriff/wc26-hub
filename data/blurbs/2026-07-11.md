The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played across 30 days of football, and the bracket is now fixed — 16 teams still alive, the rest already making travel arrangements. Today offers no pitch action, which makes it a rare pause in a tournament that has moved at a relentless pace since the opening day in June.

The knockout rounds begin tomorrow, when the first of the round-of-sixteen ties gets under way. From here the arithmetic is simple and unforgiving: lose once and go home. The accumulated points, the goal differences, the group-stage dramatics — all of it collapses into a single 90-minute reckoning, with extra time and penalties waiting in reserve if the teams cannot be separated. A rest day, then, but not a quiet one for anyone with a stake in what comes next.
