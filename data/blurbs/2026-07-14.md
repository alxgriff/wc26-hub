The group stage is done. All 72 matches have been played across 33 days, the standings are settled, and the bracket for the knockout rounds is now fixed. Tuesday offers no football — a rare pause in a tournament that has barely stopped for breath since it opened — which makes it a reasonable moment to take stock before the round of 32 begins. The teams that survived did so across a schedule stretched over more cities and time zones than any World Cup before this one, and the results were not always what the models or the pundits anticipated. Some favourites came through looking composed; others arrived at the knockout stage with questions still unanswered. The next match will clarify things considerably, as elimination tends to do.
